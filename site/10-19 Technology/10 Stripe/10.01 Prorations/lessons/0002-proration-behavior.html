Controlling proration: the three behaviours

Calculating a proration and actually billing it are two separate decisions. One setting — proration_behavior — controls both.

Lesson 0002 ~6 min The settings

Recap, then the gap

In Lesson 1 you saw that a mid-cycle change produces a credit line and a charge line. What we glossed over: when does the customer actually pay? By default Stripe makes the line items but doesn't charge right away — "positive prorations aren't immediately billed." The proration_behavior setting is how you choose what happens. It has exactly three values.

1. create_prorations — the default

Stripe creates the proration line items and parks them. They're "only invoiced immediately under certain conditions"; otherwise they ride along on the customer's next regular invoice. The customer is billed accurately, but not today.

Reach for it when: the change is an ordinary upgrade/downgrade and you're happy for the adjustment to appear on the next bill. This is the safe default.

2. always_invoice — bill it now

Stripe "calculates the proration, then immediately generates an invoice." Same credit-and-charge maths as the default — but the customer is charged for the net amount straight away rather than waiting for the next cycle.

Reach for it when: you want the cash now, or you want the customer to confirm-and-pay an upgrade in the moment (e.g. "pay $5 to upgrade today").

Failure mode worth knowing. If that immediate charge fails, the subscription change still goes through — Stripe says "the subscription change request succeeds and the subscription transitions to past_due." So the customer is on the new plan but now owes money. Finance needs to track that dunning, not assume the upgrade was clean.

3. none — skip proration entirely

No credit, no charge, no adjustment. The change takes effect and "customers are billed the full amount at the new price when the next invoice is generated." Nobody is credited for unused time and nobody is charged for the partial period.

Reach for it when: you deliberately don't want to nickel-and-dime — a goodwill plan swap, or when a trial is already absorbing the partial period (Lesson 4). Just know the customer may get more or less than they strictly paid for, in their favour or yours.

Side by side

Same upgrade as Lesson 1 ($10 → $20, halfway through the month):

create_prorations  →  −$5 credit & +$10 charge appear on the NEXT invoice
always_invoice     →  −$5 credit & +$10 charge billed NOW (net $5 today)
none               →  no proration lines; customer pays full $20 next invoice

You want a customer to pay the net upgrade cost today, the moment they switch plans. Which proration_behavior do you set?

One more nuance

Setting proration_behavior on a single change affects only that change. Setting it to none at the subscription level "turns off all future prorations for a subscription" — a standing policy, not a one-off. Know which one you (or your billing system) are doing.
