Previewing the bill before you change anything

"What will the customer actually be charged if we switch them today?" Stripe can answer that exactly — without touching the subscription.

Lesson 0003 ~5 min Forecasting

Why preview at all

You now know a change produces a credit and a charge (Lesson 1) and that you can bill it now or later (Lesson 2). The missing piece for a product or finance decision is the number itself, before you commit — to show the customer a "you'll pay $X today" confirmation, or to sanity-check a migration's revenue impact.

The preview invoice

Stripe gives you a dry run. You "create a preview invoice to preview changes to a subscription. This API call doesn't modify the subscription. Instead, it returns the upcoming invoice based only on the parameters that you pass." You hand it the proposed change (the new price), and it hands back exactly the line items that would appear — the unused-time credit and the remaining-time charge — and the total.

The thing being returned is a full upcoming invoice: the same two proration line items from Lesson 1, plus whatever else is due, totalled. Nothing is charged and nothing changes on the customer's account — it's a quote.

The catch: prorations move every second

Recall that Stripe "prorates to the second" (Lesson 1). That precision creates a subtle trap: "prorated amounts might change between the time they're previewed and the time the update is made." Preview at 2:00:00 and apply at 2:00:45 and the unused-time slice has shrunk slightly — so the quote you showed the customer no longer matches the charge.

Pinning it with proration_date

The fix is to freeze the clock. Capture one timestamp — the proration date — and pass it to both calls:

  • Pass it to the preview, so the quote is computed at that instant.
  • Pass the same value when you actually apply the change, so "the proration is calculated at the same time."

Now the quoted number and the billed number are computed from the identical moment, and they match to the cent. This is the single most important habit when you show a customer a price before they confirm.

1. proration_date = now            # capture one instant
2. preview(change, proration_date) # → show "$5.00 due today"
3. customer clicks confirm
4. apply(change, proration_date)   # same instant → charges exactly $5.00

You preview an upgrade, show the customer "$5.00 due today," then they confirm a minute later and are charged $4.97. Why, and what prevents it?
