Prorations — Cheatsheet

The compressed essence. Print it, pin it, glance at it.

The model

A mid-cycle change = credit for unused time on the old price + charge for remaining time on the new price, shown as two line items. Net them to get the amount owed.

credit = old_price × (unused_time / billing_period)   (negative)
charge = new_price × (remaining_time / billing_period) (positive)
net    = credit + charge        # >0 you owe, <0 a credit prorates back

Worked: $10 → $20, halfway through → −$5 + $10 = +$5.

Key facts

  • Prorates to the second, from the proration date (the instant of the change).
  • Line items land on the next invoice by default — not billed immediately.
  • Downgrades produce a net credit proration (signs flip).
  • Proration applies to charges billed ahead of the period; usage-based billing isn't prorated.

proration_behavior (Lesson 2)

  • create_prorations — default. Makes the line items; bills them on the next invoice.
  • always_invoice — makes the line items and invoices immediately.
  • none — no proration; customer pays the full new price next invoice.
